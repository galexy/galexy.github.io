---
title: Strongly Typed Analysis
---

** Use good (better) tools

** Don't just replicate other tools

** But, learn from them

** Create higher leveler abstractions

It's important to distinguish between conceptual and operational solutions when
solving a problem. Data analysts/scientist and software enginner, too often, conflate 
these different views of a problem domain. When that happens, these two models get tangled 
in their heads and is usually reified in their final work products, be it code or analysis.

The end result clearly resembles a thinking process that can't see the forest from the
trees. And, when these tools are reused by others, propogates a lack of clear understanding
of the real problem.
